First step was the audit. The firm's website, LinkedIn, Instagram, and YouTube each got a separate read on where they were performing and where the existing setup was leaving opportunity on the table. Two findings shaped the year's work. Boardroom Brief was producing thirty-minute long-form for YouTube and Greybrook.com, with nothing downstream for the LinkedIn and Instagram audiences that wouldn't sit through that cold. And the social post calendar was monotonous, cycling through a narrow band of fund-update and deal-announcement formats.

The content strategy that came out of the audit organized around three objectives: thought leadership, investor engagement, and brand awareness. Content types broke into market analysis, project spotlights, community engagement, and educational resources, with platform-specific treatments for LinkedIn, Instagram, and the website blog.

Execution ran across four workstreams. The video program got a social-cuts layer that took each Boardroom Brief recording from two distribution surfaces to five. The social calendar adopted a content-pillar playbook (Greybrook's View, Strategy Snapshot, Deal Spotlight, Mythbuster, among others) that broke the monotonous post cycle while keeping the editorial line. Inside Track in October was the big event of the year, with a three-phase content program built around the day. And the standard four-piece package shipped on PR Newswire whenever a deal closed.

Mid-year, I ran a Q1-vs-Q2 performance review. LinkedIn engagement rates had moved up. Instagram engagement had softened with higher posting volume. The H2 plan focused on quality over quantity, platform-native video formats, shareability-focused content like carousels and quote cards, and a tighter publishing cadence to avoid follower fatigue. Targets were specific: +150 Instagram followers and +700 LinkedIn followers, 5.0% engagement on Instagram and 12.5% on LinkedIn, and a 25% lift on combined shares.

By the year-end window I was tracking, LinkedIn engagement on Greybrook's corporate channel was up roughly 300% year-over-year, share volume up 152%, and reach up 164%.
