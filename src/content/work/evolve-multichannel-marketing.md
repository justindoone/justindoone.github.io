I'm the digital marketing manager at Evolve ETFs, reporting to the CMO. Lean team: I share an intern with the rest of marketing and otherwise own the digital footprint end-to-end. Website, social, paid media, analytics, and the in-house reporting tooling that keeps the lineup readable on a single dashboard.

Evolve runs a lineup of 44 ETFs across thematic, fixed-income, and dividend strategies. One marketing budget and one compliance posture covers them all, with each fund carrying its own audience profile and seasonal rhythm. Treating the lineup as a single campaign leaves money on the table; treating every fund as bespoke doesn't scale. The job sits in the middle.

The operating setup is built around always-on paid with campaign-level budget caps, automated creative-freshness checks on a 45-day window, weekly status to the CMO, and monthly re-anchoring against fund-level performance. I built or co-built most of the in-house tooling that holds that cadence together, since the off-the-shelf options for a Canadian ETF firm at this team size aren't a great fit.
