I'm the digital marketing manager at Evolve ETFs, reporting to the CMO. Lean team. End-to-end ownership of the digital footprint: website, social, paid media, analytics, and the in-house reporting tooling that keeps the lineup readable in one place.

Evolve runs 44 ETFs across thematic, fixed-income, and dividend strategies. One marketing budget and one compliance posture covers them all, but each fund has its own audience and its own seasonal rhythm. Treat the lineup as a single campaign and the spend gets fuzzy. Treat every fund as bespoke and the work doesn't scale.

The operating setup runs on always-on paid with campaign-level budget caps, automated creative-freshness checks on a 45-day window, weekly status to the CMO, and monthly re-anchoring against fund-level performance. Most of the tooling that holds the cadence together I built or co-built, since the off-the-shelf options for a Canadian ETF firm at this team size aren't a great fit.
