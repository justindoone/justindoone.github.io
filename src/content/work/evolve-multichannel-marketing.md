Digital marketing manager at Evolve, reporting to the CMO. Small team. End-to-end ownership of the digital footprint: website, social, paid media, analytics, and the reporting layer.

41 ETFs across thematic, fixed-income, and dividend strategies. One marketing budget covers all of them. One compliance posture. But each fund has its own audience and its own seasonal rhythm. Treat the lineup as one campaign and the spend gets fuzzy. Treat every fund as bespoke and the work doesn't scale.

The setup: always-on paid with campaign-level budget caps, automated creative-freshness checks on a 45-day window, weekly status to the CMO, monthly re-anchoring against fund-level performance.

## Tooling

A lot of what makes that cadence work is small AI-built tools that make the team more efficient. The bulk is data synthesis. Consolidated reporting pulls Google Ads, Reddit, Meta, GA4, and the WordPress site into one view, so the data-driven decisions get made from one place instead of five tabs. Competitive-intel reporting on what other Canadian ETF firms are running and saying in market, refreshed monthly.

End-of-month update tools handle the recurring chores: distribution data into the public site, performance numbers into the deck, the small repeatable jobs that otherwise eat hours every cycle.

Most of these are AI-assisted builds. Faster than waiting for off-the-shelf to fit a Canadian ETF firm at this team size.
