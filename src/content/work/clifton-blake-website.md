## The brief

Clifton Blake's old site told you *what* the firm had built. The brief was to rebuild it so the site told you *why you'd trust them with capital* — a much harder problem, and the one that actually mattered for the audience they were trying to reach.

## The approach

We started with audit, not aesthetics. Sat down with the leadership team, the IR team, and a sample of recent investors and walked through:

- What questions came up most often in pitch meetings?
- What did the firm spend the most time explaining *after* the deck?
- What were the moments where prospects went quiet and we lost momentum?

The site became the answer to those questions, in the order people actually asked them. Information architecture before visuals. Visuals before copy. Copy before final design.

### Structural shifts

- **Track record up front** — institutional readers want evidence before philosophy
- **Process before product** — how the firm sources, underwrites, and executes
- **Quiet design** — sparse layouts that earned trust through restraint, not motion

## What it produced

Organic traffic grew an order of magnitude within the first six months — partly because the site was finally indexable, partly because the content was finally answering search queries that mattered. Bounce rate dropped sharply, average session time more than tripled, and the IR team began routing prospects to specific pages of the site as part of their normal flow.

## What I'd do again

Spend the first week with the sales team, not the design team. The site exists to remove friction from a conversation that's already happening — and the people in that conversation know exactly where the friction lives.
