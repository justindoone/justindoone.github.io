## The brief

Greybrook's Inside Track is the firm's marquee investor event — one room, a few hours, an audience that doesn't fill again until the following year. The historical pattern: enormous prep effort, a strong day, and then most of the content evaporated by week's end.

The brief was simple to state and harder to execute: **make the in-room moment last a quarter, not a day.**

## The approach

We rebuilt the event as a content platform rather than a one-day broadcast. Every session was planned twice — once for the room, once for the distribution pipeline that came after.

- **Pre-event:** speaker spotlights, agenda teasers, and short-form thesis posts seeded across LinkedIn and email
- **Day-of:** real-time clips, quote cards, and behind-the-scenes shots designed for next-day publication
- **Post-event:** long-form recaps, edited keynote cuts, and a downloadable summary positioned as the "if you missed it" artifact for the entire investor universe

The shift wasn't tactical. It was treating the event itself as the *content production engine* — and the calendar afterward as the actual distribution play.

## What it produced

The event itself ran cleaner because the content team's needs were baked into the run-of-show from week one — no scrambling for B-roll, no missed soundbites. The distribution arc generated triple the post-event engagement of the prior year's program, and shifted the firm's calendar from "one big day" to a recurring content cadence.

## What I'd do again

Plan the *distribution* before you plan the agenda. The room is the input; the next sixty days are the actual product.
