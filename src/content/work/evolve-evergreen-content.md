Most of the content questions at Evolve come down to compliance. The marketing-conduct rules need interpretation, and the gap between safety and reach is where the real work happens.

The biggest current push is the YouTube content program. The firm has been treating organic YouTube videos like paid creative, pulling them down every 45 days and re-publishing fresh on the assumption that the same refresh rule that applies to ad units applies to owned-channel content. The result is that nothing on the channel accumulates value. Subscribers don't compound and search-ranking equity gets wiped every quarter and a half.

I've been arguing internally that the 45-day rule is a marketing-conduct standard for paid creative, not a blanket policy for organic content, and pushing toward an interpretation that lets evergreen videos stay live. While that conversation continues, I'm building the filming schedule and content backlog that the channel would need either way: recurring formats tied to fund themes, an editorial calendar built around quarterly market windows, and a backlog that absorbs the next compliance interpretation without restarting the work.

The same logic shows up on the AEO and schema side. ETF buyers increasingly check ChatGPT and other answer engines before they look at a fund's actual site. Most Canadian ETF issuers haven't set up the FAQ schema or structured data those engines look for. I'm building the audit layer that maps where Evolve's fund pages can be picked up, where competitors have left gaps, and what schema patterns improve placement.

The throughline is content that accumulates value rather than getting restarted by an over-cautious compliance read. The work is to set up the conditions where each piece of content does more work over time.
