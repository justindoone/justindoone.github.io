## The brief

A TV spot on BNN Bloomberg is a strong audience match for an asset manager — but a single broadcast buy is also a uniquely *inefficient* use of a marketing budget if you don't extract more from it than its airtime alone delivers.

The brief was to plan the spot from day one as the *hero asset of a much larger campaign*, not as a standalone broadcast moment.

## The approach

We built the shoot day and the post-production process around three downstream uses, not one:

- **The 30-second TV cut** — the public anchor
- **Vertical and square edits** for paid social and LinkedIn distribution
- **Stills, B-roll, and voiceover takes** that fed sales decks, the website refresh, and conference materials for the next year

Every editorial decision — pacing, framing, what the host said and didn't — was made with the multi-channel pipeline already in view.

## What it produced

The campaign extended well beyond its broadcast window — most of the value lived in the asset library that came out of the same day's production work. The same spot continued surfacing across paid social, the website hero, and IR materials for nearly a quarter after the original air date.

## What I'd do again

Don't shoot a TV ad. Shoot a *content shoot that also produces a TV ad.* Same crew, same day, ten times the downstream value.
