## The brief

Roxborough operates a mixed portfolio — residential rentals and commercial leasing. Two audiences, two sales cycles, two very different sets of questions. The old site treated them as the same person, which meant it served neither well.

The brief was to give each audience its own clear path through the site, while keeping the brand identity coherent enough that both paths still felt like the same company.

## The approach

The unlock was accepting that **the two audiences are not the same audience**, and designing accordingly. Residential prospects want photos, neighbourhoods, and a fast path to availability. Commercial prospects want square footage, drawings, building specs, and the leasing contact's email.

We split the experience cleanly at the front door but unified everything below the surface:

- Distinct landing experiences for each audience
- Shared design system, typography, and tone
- A single content backend so the team only had to maintain one site
- Lead capture optimized separately for each path

## What it produced

Lead-form completion rates improved meaningfully on both sides — particularly on the commercial side, where the prior site had been routing prospects through a residential-leaning funnel that fit them poorly. The marketing team also stopped getting misrouted inquiries, which had a quieter but real impact on cycle time.

## What I'd do again

Resist the urge to "blend" audiences when they're genuinely different. A clean fork at the front door beats a muddled middle.
