Roxborough serves two genuinely different audiences. The old site treated them as the same person, which meant it served neither well. The brief was to give each audience its own clear path through the site without the brand surface fracturing in the process.

The unlock was accepting that two different audiences need two different paths. One side wanted photos, neighbourhoods, and a fast route to availability. The other side wanted specs, drawings, square footage, and the leasing contact's email. Those aren't variations on the same job.

We split the experience at the front door but unified everything below the surface:

- Distinct landing experiences for each audience track
- Shared design system, typography, and tone
- A single content backend so the team only maintained one site
- Lead capture optimized separately for each path

Form completion rates improved meaningfully on both sides, especially on the side that had previously been routed through a funnel that fit them poorly. The marketing team also stopped getting misrouted inquiries, which had a quieter but real impact on cycle time.
